Stocks: Ownership in Companies

When you buy a stock, you buy a small piece of a company ‚Äî becoming a partial owner with rights to profits and voting.

What Are Stocks?

A stock (or share) represents equity ownership in a corporation. Publicly traded companies issue shares on stock exchanges (e.g., NYSE, Nasdaq).

  • Common Stock: Voting rights + dividends (if declared)
  • Preferred Stock: Fixed dividends, priority in liquidation, but usually no voting rights
  • Market Cap: Total value = Share Price √ó Shares Outstanding

ETFs: Instant Diversification

Exchange-Traded Funds bundle hundreds of assets into a single ticker ‚Äî trade like a stock, diversify like a pro.

What Are ETFs?

An ETF is a basket of securities (stocks, bonds, commodities) that tracks an index, sector, or strategy. Trades on exchanges in real-time like stocks.

Key Types:
  • Broad Market: VTI (Total US Stock Market), VXUS (International)
  • Sector: XLK (Tech), XLF (Financials)
  • Thematic: ARKK (Innovation), ICLN (Clean Energy)
  • Bond: BND (Total Bond Market), TLT (Long-Term Treasuries)
  • Smart Beta: QUAL (Quality), MTUM (Momentum)

Bonds: Lending for Steady Income

When you buy a bond, you lend money to a government or corporation in exchange for regular interest payments and return of principal.

What Are Bonds?

A bond is a debt security. You ‚Äî the bondholder ‚Äî are the lender. The issuer (e.g., US Treasury, Apple) is the borrower.

Key Terms:
  • Face Value: Amount repaid at maturity (usually $1,000)
  • Coupon Rate: Annual interest % (e.g., 4% = $40/year on $1,000)
  • Maturity: When principal is repaid (1yr, 10yr, 30yr)
  • Yield: Actual return (changes with market price)
  • Credit Rating: Risk assessment (AAA = safest, CCC = junk)
Bond Types Compared
Treasuries US Government | AAA | Low yield | Tax-exempt (state/local)
Munis State/City | AA | Medium yield | Federal tax-exempt
Investment-Grade Corps Corporations | BBB+ | Higher yield | Taxable
High-Yield (Junk) Corporations | BB- | Highest yield | Highest risk

How to Buy & Sell Bonds

1
Through ETFs (Recommended)

BND (Total Bond), AGG (US Aggregate), TLT (20+ Yr Treasuries). Instant diversification.

2
Direct via Broker

Buy individual bonds on Fidelity/Schwab. Minimums: $1,000. Watch for markups.

3
TreasuryDirect.gov

Buy US Treasuries directly (no fees). Great for laddering strategies.

4
Sell Before Maturity

Bonds trade on secondary markets. Price moves inversely to interest rates.

Advantages

  • Income Stability: Predictable coupon payments
  • Capital Preservation: Principal returned at maturity (if no default)
  • Low Volatility: Less price swing than stocks
  • Diversification: Bonds often rise when stocks fall (negative correlation)
  • Tax Benefits: Munis = federal tax-free; Treasuries = state tax-free

Risks

  • Interest Rate Risk: When rates ‚Üë, bond prices ‚Üì (longer maturity = higher risk)
  • Inflation Risk: Fixed payments lose purchasing power over time
  • Credit Risk: Issuer defaults (junk bonds: 2-5% annual default rate)
  • Liquidity Risk: Hard to sell individual bonds quickly without discount
  • Reinvestment Risk: Coupons reinvested at lower rates if yields fall

Cryptocurrencies: Digital Assets

Decentralized digital money and programmable assets built on blockchain technology ‚Äî enabling new financial systems.

What Is Crypto?

Cryptocurrencies are digital assets secured by cryptography and decentralized networks (blockchains). No central bank or authority controls them.

Major Categories:
  • Store of Value: Bitcoin (BTC) ‚Äî ‚Äúdigital gold‚Äù
  • Smart Contract Platforms: Ethereum (ETH), Solana (SOL)
  • Stablecoins: USDC, DAI ‚Äî pegged to $1
  • DeFi Tokens: UNI (Uniswap), AAVE (lending)
  • Meme Coins: DOGE, SHIB ‚Äî community-driven, high risk
How Blockchains Work
1. Transaction Created

You send 1 ETH to a friend.

2. Broadcast to Network

Nodes validate signature & balance.

3. Mining/Validation

Miners (PoW) or Validators (PoS) bundle tx into a block.

4. Block Added

Block appended to chain ‚Üí transaction confirmed.

DEX (Decentralized): Uniswap, PancakeSwap (self-custody)

2

3

4

Advantages

  • 24/7 Markets: Trade anytime, anywhere
  • High Growth Potential: BTC +9,000,000% since 2010

  • Programmability: Smart contracts enable DeFi, NFTs, DAOs
  • Global Access: Bank the unbanked (1.7B people without banks)
